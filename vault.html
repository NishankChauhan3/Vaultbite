🛡 Security Tips

UK-focused password security guidance

🔑
Use unique passwords everywhere
Never reuse passwords. If one site is breached, attackers will try the same credentials on other sites — known as credential stuffing. VaultBite makes this easy.
📏
Length beats complexity
A 20-character passphrase is stronger than a 10-character jumble. Aim for at least 12 characters for all passwords, and 16+ for important accounts.
📱
Enable two-factor authentication
Add a second layer beyond your password. Use an authenticator app like Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator — SMS is less secure but still better than nothing.
🚨
Check for data breaches
Visit haveibeenpwned.com (run by UK security researcher Troy Hunt) to see if your email or passwords have been exposed in known data breaches. Check regularly.
🏦
Protect financial accounts first
UK banks and HMRC accounts deserve the strongest, most unique passwords. Your Barclays, NatWest, HSBC, or HMRC login should never share a password with any other site.
🎣
Watch out for phishing
The NCSC (UK's National Cyber Security Centre) reports phishing as the most common cyberattack. Always verify URLs before logging in, especially for banking and government sites (gov.uk).
🔄
When to change passwords
The NCSC no longer recommends regular password changes unless there's a breach. Instead: use strong, unique passwords from the start, and change immediately if you suspect compromise.
🫆
Use passkeys where possible
Passkeys (biometric sign-in) are phishing-resistant and faster than passwords. Enable them on sites that support them — Google, Apple, Microsoft, and many banks now offer passkeys.
🧒
Protect children's accounts too
Young people's gaming, social, and school accounts are frequent targets. Help children set strong passwords and understand not to share them with friends.
🇬🇧 UK Resources: NCSC.gov.uk · HaveIBeenPwned.com · Action Fraud
